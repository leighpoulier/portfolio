Iguazu Falls, Brazil

Posted by Leigh on 24 Jan 2016

Mid Wednesday morning I was packed up, out of the apartment and in a taxi to Buenos Aires’ second airport ‘Aeroparque’, only about 20mins from Palermo and Centro.

I was a bit sad to be leaving BsAs, I had made some nice friends, the weather had been amazing, and I felt pretty comfortable getting around the city on the Subte (subway).

The gods of staff travel (old and new) must be smiling upon me this trip, because I haven’t been knocked back for a flight yet! Mr Aerolineas Argentineas checked me in straight away.

About two hours later we touched down in what looked like the depths of the jungle, at Puerto Iguazu airport, just inside Argentina, a stone’s throw from the borders of Paraguay and Brazil, and this is where my very luxurious 24hrs at the Cataratas Del Iguazu (Iguazu Falls) began…

A very nice man from the hotel was waiting with my name on a board on arrival. Good afternoon Mr Poulier How are you Mr Poulier? How was your flight Mr Poulier? He took my bags and loaded them into his car, where iced water and a copy of the New York times was waiting. Aaah this is how it’s supposed to be!

We drove for approximately 45 minutes and Mr Driver (I can’t remember his name), who was a local of the area, told me all about the falls, the National Park they are in, the wildlife, and the hotel. About 25 minutes into the drive we reached the border between Argentina and Brazil. First we exited Argentina, all from the comfort of the car.

We then crossed a bridge over the Rio Iguazu / Iguaçu (spanish / portuguese), and I waited in the car sipping my iced water while Mr Driver took my passport inside and took care of Brazilian entry formalities. If only immigration was always this easy. Soon enough we were on our way into the Parque Nacional Iguaçu, and the hotel. And what a hotel!

Welcome to the Belmond Hotel des Cataratas. The only hotel inside the National Park, and directly across from the falls!

Soon after check-in I was on my way along the cliff top path just opposite the hotel which gives views of the falls. Being about 530pm by this time the riff raff had been barelled out of the park (public hours 9-5) and I was left in relative solitude to get my first look.

Wow. Continuing along the path we come to the Devils Throat, the largest contiguous waterfall, and the path leads out into the water.

Nobody warned me that the ‘mist’ coming off the falls is not mist. I got slightly wet…

Then after meeting some of the local wildlife, I caught sunset over the falls.

Then back to the hotel for dinner by the pool of Brazilian Bbq Buffet, then bed. The next day I woke early and headed back to the lookout path to catch sunrise hitting the falls before the masses arrived.

I Caught the hotel shuttle into the town outside the park gates for my first order of business, helicopter ride over the falls! One great advantage of traveling alone is your can always skip the queue to fill a single space, so in just a few minutes I was up in the air, and 9 minutes later, on the ground again! But that’s ok. I got my photos and I was on a tourism mission, had to be back at the hotel by 430pm for Jeeves to drive me to the airport! Next stop, the Bird Park…

I was told by the hotel concierge that it was worth going to but I was a bit sceptical, however I was pleasantly surprised..

Ok time to go… On schedule! Back on the bus and into the park for the Macucu safari, which consisted of a ride through the jungle, a trek to the river, donning a lifejacket, into a speedboat, and out onto Rio Iguazu, and into the falls.

So the safari was pretty cool. At least this time I knew I was going to get drenched, and I didn’t bring the big camera. I was a little worried about my phone, and at one point I thought it had died, but it was just too sunny to see the screen!

Then Jeeves drove me to the airport, and again the staff travel gods smiled on me as I was successful in getting on two connecting flights, first to São Paulo, then to Maceió on the north east coast of Brazil.
